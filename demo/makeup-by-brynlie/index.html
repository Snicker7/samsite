About Brynlie

The chair where the morning slows down.

I started doing makeup in my Provo apartment the year I got married — sitting friends down on a stool in front of the bathroom mirror because I didn't have anywhere else to put them. That was 2019. I have a real studio now, but the feel of that room hasn't changed: slow, chatty, lots of natural light, a second cup of tea if you want one.

My work leans soft-glam. Satin skin, a defined but not-heavy lash, a lip that looks like yours. I take a small number of weddings a year on purpose — I'd rather do thirty weddings really, really well than sixty on autopilot.

— Brynlie

§ 02 · About

A working artist in a small studio north of salt lake.

Brynlie Peay started this practice in 2019, the same year she got married. Seven seasons in, the work has a shape: soft-glam bridal makeup that photographs cleanly on film, holds through a twelve-hour day, and doesn't look like every other bride's.

She takes no more than thirty weddings a year. The short roster is how the morning of the wedding gets to feel the way it's supposed to — slow, unhurried, maybe a second cup of coffee before the first brush comes out.

Her studio is in Lehi, twenty minutes south of Salt Lake. Travel is included across the Wasatch Front; destination weddings by conversation.
