Where the West says "I do" — five ways

Black Iron Ranch, five ways.

Five different feels for the same site. Same property, same story, same sections — but each direction carries a different mood. The first is a refined evolution of your current Squarespace site. The next four push further: a cinematic film treatment, a vintage almanac, a planner's working sketchbook, and a forged-iron industrial. Scroll through all five, then tell me which one feels most like the ranch you built — and what to borrow from the others.

Our Story

Two friends. One barn.
Twenty acres of sky.

Black Iron Ranch was built by two lifelong friends who wanted to preserve the magic of their own country childhood — long tables of people they loved, dust on everyone's shoes, and a horizon you could not see the end of.

~ chapter three · a day here

What a wedding day looks like, roughly.

Every couple writes their own timeline — but here's the rhythm a typical full-day rental tends to follow.

10am

Arrival + bridal suite

Doors unlock. Florals arrive. Hair + makeup set up in the bridal suite — east light through the windows.

2pm

Vendor load-in

Caterer takes the prep kitchen. Photographer scouts the pasture. DJ runs sound check in the barn.

our hostess walks vendors through everything — you don't have to be the project manager today
4:30

First look + portraits

Bridal-party portraits in the back pasture, golden light, no one rushing you.

5:30

Ceremony

Aisle pointed west. Guests facing the sunset. We line everyone up for you.

6:30

Cocktails + dinner

Cocktail hour outside, dinner inside, long tables and string lights overhead.

beer + wine + champagne approved · up to two specialty drinks
9:30

Sparkler send-off

Sparklers permitted (outside fire bans). We line both sides of the walk.

10pm

Curfew + load-out

Music down, sparklers out. Vendors break down. We lock up. You head home.
